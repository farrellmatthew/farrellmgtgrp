Section One

The Brand

We don't sell a better golf game. We sell the part of golf that happens between the golf.

The Insight

The existing golf cannabis brands all sell performance — "focus, calm, swing tempo." Our customer doesn't want help playing better. He plays plenty well. What he wants is the buzz that fits the round, the turn, and the long Saturday after. Wellness brands are not talking to him. We are.

The Positioning

Halfway-house-coded weed gummy for guys who'd rather be at the turn than the tavern.

The Promise

The right buzz for the round, the turn, and the long Saturday after — without the hangover or the headache. Two for the turn. One for the porch.

The Pillars

  1. Insider, not outsider. If you have to ask what the turn is, the brand isn't for you.
  2. Discreet, not loud. The tin sits on the kitchen counter when his mother visits. No cannabis iconography — ever.
  3. Send-it, but answer your mother's call. Loose with the boys, polished in public, never apologizing for either.
  4. Heirloom-coded, post-college honest. Looks like 1965. Feels like 2026.

Section Two

The Audience

He's 26. He went to one of the schools. The uniform's been the same since rush week sophomore year.

Commercial real estate, medical device sales, or "Dad's company" — the answer is always vague because the answer is always "I make more than you'd guess." Says ma'am and sir to anyone over 40. Says brotherrr to anyone under it. Drives a charcoal Tahoe. Plays in salmon shorts. Owns a Yeti for every occasion. Has been to more weddings in the last three years than most people attend in a lifetime.

He doesn't drink at bars — not because he's sober, but because he genuinely cannot stand a sticky floor or a cloud of smoke parked outside the door, and he'll tell you so with the conviction of a man delivering a sermon. He saves the bourbon for weddings, the beer for tailgates, and the rest of the time he's two-deep on the leather recliner with the SEC slate on. He has opinions on Chip Kelly, on smash burger doneness, on the correct width of a tie. The infuriating part is he's mostly right.

Respectfully, brotherrr — why are we standing in a puddle when we could be on my back porch?
Section Three

How We Talk

The Voice Principle

Yes-sir manners with a send-it engine underneath.

Every line of copy passes the same test: would the persona send it in his groomsmen group chat and would his mother smile at it on the kitchen counter? Both yes — ship it. Either no — hold it.

Voice Rules

  1. Insider, not explainer. Never define "the turn" or "halfway house." If you know, you know.
  2. Southern lawyer cadence for the jokes. "Respectfully, brotherrr" energy.
  3. Self-aware without leaning in. The frat is in the bones, not the labels.
  4. Discreet on cannabis. No "high," no "lifted." Say two-deep, paceable, the right amount.
  5. Respect what shouldn't be sent. The brand acknowledges Sundays, mothers, weddings, and work.
  6. Short, with rhythm. Read every line out loud. If it doesn't have cadence, cut it.

Section Five

House Rules

Before any piece of content ships — written, designed, photographed, posted — it goes through the one-sentence test.

Would this work on the leather recliner with the boys Saturday afternoon — and on the kitchen counter when his mother walks in Monday morning?

Both yes: ship it. Either no: hold it. No exceptions, no overrides, no "this one time."

Do

  • Real guys, cast from the persona
  • Golden hour, overcast, soft window light
  • Cart paths, halfway-house counters, back porches
  • One persona word per composition
  • "Two-deep," "paceable," "right amount"
  • Mock-formal Southern when joking
  • Tin on the console. Sleeve in the bag.

Don't

  • Models. Influencers in branded fits.
  • Midday studio light. Ring lights.
  • Pristine fairways. White golf balls on tees.
  • Stacking three slang words in one line
  • "High," "stoned," "lit," "420," "blazed"
  • Sarcasm at the audience's expense
  • Floating product on a teal gradient
Section Six

The Product

Nano-emulsified. THC + CBD at two-to-one. Paceable by design.

Fast-onset (10–20 minutes) means it lands with the moment, not three holes later. Short duration (2–4 hours) means it clears before he drives home from dinner. The 2:1 ratio with CBD smooths the curve — no paranoia spike, no overshoot, no "I'm too high" moments. CBG is deliberately absent. CBG is the wellness signal Greenside and Bite both lean on; we don't.

SKU Architecture

The Foursome Tin — four gummies, the cart-bag standard. One per player. The unit the brand was designed around.

The Saturday Tin — eight gummies. The host's tin. Sits on the kitchen counter when the boys come over.

The Singles Sleeve — individually wrapped, granola-bar discreet from the outside. For the golf bag, the boat cooler, the rehearsal-dinner blazer pocket.

Section Seven

The Pitch

What you walk in with when you sit down across from a state-licensed cannabis operator. Ninety seconds, max.

There are three brands going after golfers with THC, and they all sound the same — focus, calm, wellness. None of them are talking to the actual customer: the 25-to-35-year-old Southern guy who plays a lot of golf, hosts the boys, and has already substituted gummies for most of his weeknight bourbon.

We've built the brand for him. Name, voice, design, persona, ritual, product spec. The product is nano-emulsified THC + CBD at a paceable 2:1 ratio, designed to clear before he drives home. We want to partner with an operator who manufactures and distributes. We own the IP and the brand story. You get a defensible, differentiated SKU in a category your other brands aren't reaching.
